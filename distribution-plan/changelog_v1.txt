# Fadeo v1.0.0 - First public release

Fadeo plays, pauses, and switches your Mac's audio automatically based on what you are doing,
and shows you why. This is the first public build. It is free and open source (GPLv3).

## 🎧 What it does

### Context-aware switching
- Reacts to the frontmost app, the desktop Space you are on, whether your camera or mic is in
  use (a meeting), your Focus mode, and the time of day.
- Build "workspaces" with rules. When several match, an ordered tiebreak chain (stickiness,
  specificity, priority, stable order) resolves a single winner, and you control the order.
- Mark an app as "weak" so it keeps a workspace playing but never yanks you into it, which is
  how shared apps like Slack or Notes stop hijacking your sound.
- Override workspaces (for example Meetings) pre-empt everything the moment they match.

### It tells you why
- The Now screen shows the active workspace, what it is doing to your audio, the live context
  (frontmost app, Space, meeting, Focus), and the exact reason a workspace won.

### Your sound, or its own
- On-device ambient textures synthesized in real time: brown, pink, and white noise, plus
  rain, ocean, wind, and fan. No audio files shipped, no looping seams.
- Or conduct Spotify and Apple Music, including a specific playlist, with per-source volume.
- Bring your own files, folders, or a curated local playlist, with order and repeat controls.

### Built to disappear
- Event driven with no polling, so it sits near 0% CPU at idle. Sensors only run when a rule
  needs them. Meeting detection needs no camera or microphone permission prompt.
- Never touches your system volume. Each workspace has its own level.
- Tunable fades, crossfades, enter and exit delays, and minimum dwell, so the audio never
  thrashes on rapid app switching.
- Resume across quit: internal files and Apple Music or Spotify pick up where they left off.
- Usage screen with time and switches per workspace, and a fully opt-in, coarse diagnostics
  summary if you want to help.

## 🐛 Known limitations (being honest)
- No Apple Developer ID yet, so the app is ad-hoc signed. The first launch needs a right-click
  then Open to clear Gatekeeper.
- Detecting the desktop Space index uses a private API that can change between macOS releases;
  if it ever breaks, app-based rules keep working and it degrades to "space changed, index
  unknown".
