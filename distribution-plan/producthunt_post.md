# Product Hunt

**Mechanics:** set up the Upcoming page about a week ahead so people can subscribe. You can
hunt your own product. Launches open at 12:01am Pacific and run 24 hours. Reply to every
comment. Never ask for or organize upvotes. For a solo indie, a Saturday launch tends to have
less competition than Tuesday to Thursday.

**Name:** Fadeo

**Tagline:**
Automatic ambient audio for macOS that follows what you're doing

**Description:**

Fadeo is a native macOS menu bar app that plays, fades, and switches your audio based on context: the frontmost app, the desktop Space, whether your camera or mic is in use (meetings pause your audio automatically), your Focus mode, and time of day. It shows you why it chose a given sound, so the automation is never a mystery.

For sound it generates ambient textures on device (brown, pink, white noise, rain, ocean, wind, fan, no audio files), or it conducts Spotify and Apple Music, including specific playlists. It is event driven, so it stays near 0% CPU at idle, and it never touches your system volume, so each workspace has its own level while your volume keys stay yours.

Free and open source (GPLv3). A pay-what-you-want lifetime license ($2 minimum) only removes a small occasional reminder, and the first 100 are free. macOS 14 and up. Note: it is ad-hoc signed for now (no Developer ID yet), so the first launch needs a right-click then Open.

**First comment (maker):**

Maker here. I built Fadeo to stop juggling background audio by hand: starting noise when I focus, killing it for calls, swapping it when I move desktops. It reads cheap OS signals and does the switching itself, and it tells you why.

Two choices I care about. It is event driven with no polling, so it stays invisible in Activity Monitor. And it never controls your system volume, each workspace has its own level.

It is open source and free, and the first 100 lifetime licenses are free on the site. Honest limit up front: no Apple Developer ID yet, so the first open needs a right-click then Open. There is a short demo on the site if you would rather see it before downloading. Happy to answer anything.
